Irys uses a protocol-level treasury to account for future storage costs.

Irys' protocol treasury ensures the sustainability of storage costs through upfront payments, an inflationary schedule, and mechanisms to maintain the value of the treasury. These measures protect against token dilution and ensure that storage providers are incentivized to maintain data storage over long periods.

These features make Irysâ€™ protocol treasury a robust system for managing decentralized storage costs, aligning incentives for storage providers, and safeguarding the network's economic stability.

## Payment for Storage

When users pay for storage in Irysâ€™ [data ledgers](/protocol/ledgers), they make a single upfront payment. This payment is based on the amount of data uploaded and the cost of storing a [partition](/protocol/partitions) (a storage unit provided by miners on Irys) for a year. Users posting data to Irysâ€™ permanent ledger pay for 200 years in advance, assuming a 1% annual decline in storage costs.

## Inflation

Irys has an inflationary token emission schedule (similar to Bitcoin). A fixed amount is emitted each year through block rewards. Unlike Bitcoin's specific halving events, the inflation rate on Irys gradually decreases with each block.

## Block Rewards

Block rewards on Irys are tracked per [partition](/protocol/partitions) and linked to the storage pricing model. The rewards are designed to cover the cost of storing a partition for a year. If block rewards fall below the necessary amount, tokens from the treasury will cover the shortfall to ensure miners can maintain their operations, which is crucial for the network's fault tolerance.
Initially, inflationary rewards might be excessively high, causing unfair dilution of token holders in favor of miners. When per-partition rewards exceed a certain fiat value (as determined by Irysâ€™ Price Adjustment Mechanism), excess tokens are added to the treasury. This increases the treasury's purchasing power and applies deflationary pressure on the circulating supply of the token.

## Anti-Dilution Measures

Inflation increases the token supply over time, slowly diluting the value of tokens currently in circulation and in the treasury. Since the treasury's token value is used to pay for future data storage, maintaining its value is crucial. To combat dilution, the treasury receives a proportional amount of new tokens equivalent to the annual inflation rate, ensuring its purchasing power is maintained relative to the overall supply.
